LIGHTING OF THE BMW 6 COUPÉ

STUDIOLIGHTS AND STUDIO ENVIRONMENT

GROUND:

The Ground is quite important. It should be as neutral as possible. In general I use the standart Material – with plain white color. No specular color – just a matte white floor.

One major aspect is to create a surface that doesn´t have any visible edges, cause it won´t look that good if you can see the part where the wall hits the floor. In this area the shadows would "jump" from one to the other surface. Even the color would be different – this often creates a separation line.

This ground has a round surface – it blends from vertical to horizontal surface.

The bended surface gives you the possibility to move the cam around without showing the real environment – in general, there is nothing in 3d space – in a real photosdudio you would have the actual walls of the building.

REFLECTIONS

As we imagine that we create an interior environment we have to create geometry that can be reflected.

For exterior scenes you often use spherical maps. Nowadays you can use HDRI files which give you additional to the correct light also the correct reflections.

The paint material of a car "lives" for the reflection. Other then diffuse materials like fabrics, wood or any other material a reflective material shows the environment.

If there would be no reflections it´s would be hard for your eyes to "see" the surface and it´s curves.

The reflections bend the surface and give them a "direction".

As we don´t have a real environment like for a photoshooting, we have to create additional geometry that can be reflected.

In fact we have the same approach as a photographer would do.

…for chris…well, I don´t know the English word for that, so I call it "Lichtwannen"

Licht = light

Wannen = like In the bathroom the tub

The Lichtwannen are simple geometry which only has to be reflected. They are placed around the object or behind the cam.

In music videos you can often see those light circles in the eyes of the artist – they simply placed lights around the camera in which the artist looks – this creates the additional reflection in the eyes.

If you use custom shapes you can add more "action".

LIGHTS

Here we can also use the real-photoshooting approach.

Simply add lightsources to illuminate the scene.

TIP: use photometric files. They have the huge advantage, that they have a fix lightsetup – multiplier, lightcones, shadows are all set and ready to use. Even attenuation is set, so you won´t need to pick every lightsource and adjust the settings.

I used the standart max-photometric lights – halogen

If you use photometric files from light manufactors like "Erco" you get specific optic results.

The perfectly simulate the different lightcones and behaviours.
